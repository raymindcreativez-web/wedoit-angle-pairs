The Intersection Dilemma

Why do angles matter in the real world?

Imagine you are a city planner tasked with redesigning a major traffic intersection.

If two roads cross each other and one angle is 40°, how sharp are the turns for the other vehicles? If the angles are too sharp, trucks can't turn. If they are too wide, pedestrians have to cross too far.

"By knowing just ONE angle in an intersection, geometry allows us to instantly calculate all the others without ever pulling out a protractor."

Let's find out how!

Definition of Terms

The language of geometry.

Angle

A figure formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint.

Vertex

The common endpoint where two rays meet to form an angle.

Ray (Side)

A line with a single endpoint that extends infinitely in one direction. Forms the sides of an angle.

Degrees (°)

The standard unit of measurement used to quantify the size or openness of an angle.

Congruent (≅)

Having the exact same measure. If Angle A is 50° and Angle B is 50°, they are congruent.

Intersection

The point where two or more lines cross each other, forming multiple angles.

Geometry Fundamentals

Let's build an angle.

An angle is formed by two rays (sides) sharing a common endpoint — called the vertex.

Angles are measured in degrees (°).

Meet the Angle Pairs

Overview of 5 key relationships. Click any card to explore!

Adjacent

Share a common vertex and side, no overlap.

Complementary

Two angles whose measures add up to exactly 90°.

Supplementary

Two angles whose measures add up to exactly 180°.

Linear Pair

Adjacent AND supplementary. Forms a straight line.

Vertical

Opposite angles at an intersection. Always equal.

Adjacent Angles

Next-door neighbors

Adjacent angles share a common vertex and a common side (ray), but their interiors do not overlap.

They are placed side-by-side. The total angle is the sum of the two smaller adjacent angles.

60° + 60° = 120° Total

Drag to move the Shared Side.

Complementary Angles

Sum exactly to 90°

Two angles are complementary when their measures add up to exactly 90° — together they form a perfect right angle.

45° + 45° = 90°

Drag the slider to adjust the dividing ray.

Supplementary Angles

Sum exactly to 180°

Supplementary angles are two angles whose measures sum to exactly 180°.

They do not need to be attached! No matter where they are, if they add to 180°, they are supplementary.

50° + 130° = 180°

Drag the slider to adjust the pair.

Linear Pair

Adjacent + Supplementary

A Linear Pair is a pair of adjacent angles formed by intersecting lines.

Because they are supplementary (sum to 180°) and adjacent (share a side), their non-shared sides form a perfect straight line.

130° + 50° = 180°

Drag to change the shared side of the line.

Vertical Angles

Opposite angles are ALWAYS equal.

Rotate the intersecting line to see the relationships.

Orange Pair (Vertical) 50° = 50°
Green Pair (Vertical) 130° = 130°

Intersection Solved!

Applying Angle Pairs in Real Life

  • 1

    Vertical Angles: The angle directly across from 40° is its vertical pair, making it exactly 40°.

  • 2

    Linear Pairs: The road is a straight line (180°). The angle adjacent to 40° forms a linear pair: 180° - 40° = 140°.

  • 3

    The Final Angle: The last angle is vertical to 140°, making it 140° as well.

Problem Solved! All 4 corners calculated purely from a single 40° measurement.
